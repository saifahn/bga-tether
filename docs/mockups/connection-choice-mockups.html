Issue #34 · Connect Astronauts UX

Choosing between two valid connection spots

The scenario from the issue: your current group is the long column 22–30 (with the 24·14·04 arm), and you want to merge in the 31·21 group. Both 22 ↔ 21 and 30 ↔ 31 are legal — and they produce different shapes: one is 4 wide, the other 3 wide. Width is what the horizontal player scores, so the choice is worth a point. Three ways the UI could offer that choice — all of them only appear when there is more than one valid spot; a single valid spot connects immediately as today. Every demo below is clickable.

AHighlight & click a card

Every card that gives a distinct connection pulses gold. Clicking the 21 commits the 22 ↔ 21 merge; clicking the 31 commits 30 ↔ 31. This is the game's existing interaction vocabulary — merges already work by clicking a card in the other group — so the only change is making which card you click meaningful and prompting the player when it matters.

Try it: click the 21 or the 31.

  • Smallest change — reuses the selectable-card pattern and current handlers.
  • Player must infer the resulting shape in their head before clicking.

BConfirmation dialog

The player clicks the group they want to merge (either card), and a dialog lists the possible connections with their resulting dimensions. Nothing happens on the board until they confirm.

Try it: click either card in the 31·21 group.

  • Explicit — can state the score consequence (“4 wide” vs “3 wide”) in words.
  • A modal interrupts board flow; text like “22 ↔ 21” is less direct than pointing at the board.

CGhost previews

The board shows a dashed ghost of the incoming group at each valid landing spot. The player clicks the ghost they want. What you see is exactly what you get — no mental rotation required.

Try it: click one of the dashed ghost cards.

  • Clearest — the layout (and therefore the score) is visible before committing.
  • Biggest change: the board must render empty/ghost cells, which the current DOM doesn't do.

Where this applies. The same pattern covers both ambiguous cases: merging groups (shown here) and placing a single card from hand/adrift when the current group contains both neighbours of its number (e.g. the group holds a 20 and a 22 and you play the 21 — reachable when the opponent's horizontal rows bend the group around). In placeCard the candidates are cards in your own group; the interaction is identical.
